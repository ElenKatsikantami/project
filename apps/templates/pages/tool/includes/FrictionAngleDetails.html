Description about Friction Angle Calculation

Friction angle together with cohesion define the shear strength parameters of soil. For granular / cohesionless soil (c = 0) friction angle is mainly estimated either from laboratory tests or from correlations with N SPT number.
There are many correlations to estimate friction angle for granular material from N SPT values . Their are based on the uncorrected N SPT (field measurements) or based on the corrected (N1)60 value.

Method 1 : Peck, Hanson and Thornburn (1974)

\(\phi' = 27.1+0.3N'+0.00054N'^2\)

Method 2 : defines friction angle from curve fitting of plot from Peck, Hanson and Thornburn as presented in CIRIA Report 143 (1995)

Method 3 : calculated as the average between two curve fitted curves from Terzaghi, Peck & Mersi (1996)

Method 4 and 5 : assume linear interpolation to the values defined as approximation from two different sources: Peck, Hanson and Thornburn and Meyerhof.
